Boer Goat Farming (Meat Production)

Category: Ruminants | Output: High-Quality Meat

The Boer goat is known for its fast growth rate and hardiness, making it ideal for diverse environments, especially semi-arid regions.

üõ†Ô∏è Key Requirements

  • Sturdy perimeter fencing (electric is often required).
  • Dry, draft-free shelter (a simple shed is sufficient).
  • High-quality roughage (hay, browse, shrubs).
  • Mineral block supplements (Copper is crucial).

üåç Environmental Suitability

**Arid/Semi-Arid & Scrubland:** Goats thrive in dry, hilly areas with ample woody plants (browsing) and are highly resistant to heat. Poorly suited for very wet, muddy pastures (prone to foot rot).

üí∞ Value Proposition

High feed efficiency, producing lean, premium-priced meat. They are excellent "clean-up" animals, consuming vegetation cattle won't touch, and require relatively low capital outlay.

üìò Ruminant Management Guide

  1. **Housing and Fencing:** Build secure fencing (4-5 $\text{ft}$ high) and provide simple, dry shelter raised off the ground to prevent moisture issues.
  2. **Nutrition (The Browser):** Ensure access to browse (shrubs, tree leaves) as primary diet. Supplement only during breeding or severe scarcity with grain.
  3. **Health Management:** Implement a regular deworming schedule (critical). Check feet regularly for foot rot, especially after heavy rain periods.
  4. **Breeding Cycle:** Boers can breed year-round. Manage bucks and does separately and ensure does have adequate nutrition for kidding.
  5. **Marketing:** Target local markets, ethnic stores, or direct-to-consumer sales for best meat prices.
